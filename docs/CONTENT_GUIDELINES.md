## üìä Assessment Philosophy

### Question Design Principles

**Good Questions:**
- Test understanding, not memory
- Have ONE clearly correct answer
- Options are plausible but distinctly wrong
- No negative marking in phrasing
- Relate to textbook content

**Question Difficulty:**
- **Easy:** Direct recall from textbook
- **Medium:** Application of concept
- **Hard:** Analysis or synthesis

**Example:**

**Easy:** "Sound is generated by _____."
**Medium:** "Why does sound travel faster in solids than in air?"
**Hard:** "If a pendulum's length is doubled, how does its frequency change?"

### Feedback Strategy

**For Correct Answers:**
- Praise: "Excellent!" "Well done!"
- Reinforce: Explain WHY it's correct
- Build confidence

**For Wrong Answers:**
- Gentle: "Not quite, but good thinking!"
- Explain: What's wrong and why
- Guide: Point towards correct reasoning
- Encourage: "Let's review this concept"

## ‚úèÔ∏è Exercise Question Guidelines

### Conversion Rules

**Textbook Exercise ‚Üí AI Tutor Format**

**1. Fill in the Blanks:**
```
Textbook: "The unit of frequency is _____."

AI Tutor MCQ:
Question: "The unit of frequency is _____."
Options: ["Hertz", "Decibel", "Meter", "Second"]
Correct: "A" (Hertz)
Explanation: "Frequency is measured in Hertz (Hz). 1 Hz means one oscillation per second."
```

**2. Match the Pairs:**
```
Textbook:
A: Flute ‚Üí 1: Vibrations in air

AI Tutor MCQ:
Question: "Which instrument produces sound through vibrations in the air?"
Options: ["Tabla", "Flute", "Sitar", "Veena"]
Correct: "B" (Flute)
Explanation: "Flute creates sound by vibrating the air column inside it."
```

**3. Give Scientific Reasons:**
```
Textbook: "Why did people listen for trains by putting ear to rail?"

AI Tutor MCQ:
Question: "In earlier times, people listened for distant trains by putting their ear to the rail because:"
Options: [
  "Sound travels faster in solids than in air",
  "Rails amplify sound",
  "It was a tradition",
  "Rails are always vibrating"
]
Correct: "A"
Explanation: "Sound travels much faster through solid materials (like metal rails) compared to air, so the train's vibrations reach faster through the rail."
```

### Answer Explanation Format

**Structure:**
1. State the correct answer
2. Explain WHY it's correct (concept)
3. Optional: Why other options are wrong

**Example:**
"Frequency is measured in Hertz (Hz). One Hertz means one complete oscillation happens in one second. Decibel (dB) measures sound level, not frequency."
